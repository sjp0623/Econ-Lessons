Section 1 of 6 — Questions 1–3
Federal Reserve & Monetary Policy
The Fed controls interest rates and the money supply. It uses monetary policy to keep the economy stable — not too hot, not too cold.
Question 1
What is the main job of the Federal Reserve?
Question 2
Inflation is rising fast — prices are going up every month. What should the Fed do?
Question 3 — CAHSR Connection 🚄
California High-Speed Rail borrowed billions of dollars to fund construction. The Fed then raised interest rates significantly. What is the most likely economic effect on the CAHSR project?
Section 2 of 6 — Questions 4–6
Fiscal Policy
Fiscal policy is how the government — Congress and the President — uses taxes and spending to influence the economy. It works alongside monetary policy but uses different tools.
Question 4
What is the key difference between monetary policy and fiscal policy?
Question 5 — Gun Laws Connection ⚖️
California spends state tax money on gun violence prevention programs, buyback programs, and law enforcement training related to gun regulations. What type of policy is this an example of?
Question 6
The economy is in a recession. The government wants to boost it using fiscal policy. Which action fits that goal?
Section 3 of 6 — Questions 7–8
Supply & Demand
Prices aren't random. They're the result of how much of something exists (supply) and how badly people want it (demand). Where both meet is called the equilibrium price.
Question 7 — Gun Laws Connection ⚖️
California passes a law banning several models of semi-automatic rifles, reducing the number of legal guns available for sale. According to supply and demand, what most likely happens to the price of the guns that are still legal to buy?
Question 8 — CAHSR Connection 🚄
CAHSR sets its train ticket price at $200 per ride. At that price, trains run almost empty — most people choose to drive or fly instead. What does this tell us about the $200 price?
Section 4 of 6 — Questions 9–10
Opportunity Cost & Decision Making
Every choice has a cost — not just in money, but in what you gave up. The opportunity cost is the value of the next best option you didn't pick.
Question 9 — CAHSR Connection 🚄
California has spent over $12 billion on CAHSR so far. What is the best definition of the opportunity cost of that decision?
Question 10 — Gun Laws Connection ⚖️
California dedicates significant police time and state budget to enforcing its gun laws — background checks, prosecutions, and training. An economist says this has an opportunity cost. What do they mean?
Section 5 of 6 — Questions 11–12
GDP
GDP (Gross Domestic Product) is the total value of all goods and services produced in a country in one year. It's the main way we measure whether an economy is growing or shrinking.
Question 11 — CAHSR Connection 🚄
The federal government spends $3 billion on CAHSR construction this year. How does this affect US GDP?
Question 12
A student says: "GDP going up always means life is getting better for everyone." Which statement best challenges this idea?
Section 6 of 6 — Questions 13–15
Economic Philosophies
Every economic decision comes down to one big question: how much should the government be involved? Different economic philosophies answer that differently.
Question 13
A politician argues: "The government should not be funding CAHSR. Private companies would build it faster and cheaper if we got out of the way." Which economic philosophy does this reflect?
Question 14 — Gun Laws Connection ⚖️
An economist argues that California's strict gun laws are justified because the economic cost of gun violence — ER bills, lost productivity, incarceration — is too high for society to ignore. Which philosophy best supports this reasoning?
Question 15 — Putting It All Together 🚄⚖️🏛️
California has the highest income taxes in the US, funds CAHSR through government spending, and has the strictest gun regulations in the country. Which combination of economic philosophies best describes California's overall economic approach?

✓ Free market view: regulation restricts supply, raises prices, limits individual freedom — government should stay out

✓ Student doesn't need to agree with either — just show they understand what each philosophy argues
